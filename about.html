What is “QOL” and why is there a project for it?

It’s Fall 2018. In the first floor of Villanova’s library, my pal Megan and I decide that the typical “what’s up?” or “how’s your day?” typically said in passing across campus is boring. It doesn’t prompt the sort of human interaction we are looking for. We want more impactful conversation, probably because we are Communication majors. We play with the idea until we land on a new phrase: “how’s your quality of life?” It’s so uncharacteristic and – frankly – overkill for typical small talk that respondents simply have no choice but to deliver an especially thoughtful response to match. Ridiculous as it is, in time, “quality of life” catches on with our friends and becomes more than a spoof. It becomes so normalized that I decide to take it further.


That’s a true story. Kind of elaborate to make up anyway. And it doesn’t stop there.


The “quality of life” concept finally left the library in December 2018 as The Quality of Life Project (@theqolproject), an Instagram page for sharing the answers to the question, starting at Villanova. I began to meet with other students I suspected would have something cool to say. Originally, I wanted to keep the project to myself, private and exclusive to my friends, because I never thought it could go this far. But as interest grew, so did QOL, and within a month The QOL Project had featured more than 20 unique stories. Now, it has evolved into this very site. Pretty cool.

What’s the ultimate goal of The QOL Project?

There isn’t one, besides continuing to deliver stories from as many people as possible. I could never have guessed where QOL would go when I started it and I still don’t think I could tell you what direction it will take in the future. That’s the fun part: there is no endgame, although my greatest hope is to keep growing QOL beyond where it started, especially out of the library.
